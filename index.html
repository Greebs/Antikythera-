The Mechanism

Two thousand years ago, a bronze clockwork sank off the coast of Antikythera. Recovered in 1901, it remains the oldest known analogue computer — gears encoding the arithmetic of the heavens. This tool inherits that ambition: to make the hidden structure of number navigable, visible, and in motion.

The Logic

Every integer in a 2^N interval maps to an angle by its base-2 logarithm. Multiplication becomes angular addition — and octave reduction makes that addition modular, folding every product back into the same circle. The result is a closed, self-contained system of arithmetic that mirrors the structure of musical pitch: the octave as circle, intervals as rotations, every integer in a permanent relationship with every other.

Two positions in this space are non-arbitrary: the identity at the octave boundary, and √2 at the exact midpoint — the geometric mean, the tritone, the diabolus in musica. The colour gradient runs between them. Everything else is what follows.

Views

Zoetrope. The primary view. The torus is the multiplication table in three dimensions — the major circle sweeps through rows (animated by the frame slider or play button), the minor circle encodes each product’s position within the octave. Enter fullscreen for the full experience. The toolbar inside gives access to shape (torus / horn / sphere), spin, camera angle, helix mode, display mode, and colour. N controls how many integers are in the octave; f(x) selects the column function. Press Build after changing either.

Phase. The same data as a circular chord diagram, animated row by row. Each integer on the circle maps to its product under f(x) — a chord from input angle to output angle. Midpoint mode shows the landing position of each product as a particle; its colour encodes proximity to √2. The ring of numbers around the circle are the octave integers. The gold dot marks the current frame’s input value.

Reticulum. All frames superimposed simultaneously — the complete multiplicative orbit of the chosen function across the entire octave. Where Phase shows one moment, Reticulum shows the whole. Opacity controls how much each layer contributes; at low opacity the density structure becomes visible as a continuous field.

Antikythera. A live product calculator. Place nodes anywhere on the octave circle by dragging. Products between nodes are computed in real time and drawn as chords — each chord’s angle encodes the product’s position, and its colour encodes proximity to √2. Crucially, angles here carry weight: a node placed near the identity (the octave boundary) produces products that cluster near the identity; a node near √2 produces products at maximum distance from it. The angle is not just a label — it is the arithmetic.

Atlas. A prime composition wheel. Each integer in the octave is plotted by its prime factorisation — specifically by omega order, the count of prime factors with multiplicity. Primes appear at omega=1 (red), semiprimes at omega=2, and so on. Fine mode shows the current octave; Coarse extends outward; Atlas mode places all octaves on concentric rings. The wheel can spin and lock to the current frame.

Data panel. The heatmap (bottom-left tab) shows the full product matrix as a colour grid — rows are input values, columns are f(x) outputs, colour encodes octave position. The frame profile beneath it shows the current row as a vertical chord diagram.

Controls

Colour encodes inflection proximity throughout — blue at the identity, orange at √2, with a smooth gradient between. The Colour panel adjusts hue and brightness of each endpoint independently. Rainbow mode replaces this with a continuous spectral gradient. The INF size bias slider scales particle size proportionally to colour — inflection-proximate particles grow or shrink relative to identity particles. Transparency, dot shape, and point size apply globally across all views.

The Mechanism

Two thousand years ago, a bronze clockwork sank off the coast of Antikythera. Recovered in 1901, it remains the oldest known analogue computer — gears encoding the arithmetic of the heavens. This tool is named for that same ambition: to complete the journey. What was lost is made navigable again.

The Logic

Mimicking music, this numerical arrangement turns multiplication into addition. Any integer within a 2^N interval can be mapped to an angle by its logarithm. Products of two such integers, when reduced back into the same interval, land at the sum of their angles — modular arithmetic in logarithmic space. The result is a closed, finite system where every multiplication folds back onto the same circle.

The interval 2^N is not arbitrary. It is the octave — the most universal and recognisable boundary in acoustics. Within it, integers fall at positions corresponding exactly to musical pitch. The structure is not an analogy to music; it is the same mathematics, made visible.

Two positions in this space are non-arbitrary: the identity at the boundary, and √2 — the geometric mean, the tritone, the diabolus in musica — at the midpoint. The colour gradient runs between them. Everything else follows.

How to Use

For the best experience, open Zoetrope fullscreen. Set N and f(x), press Build, then press play. The torus sweeps through the multiplication table in motion — each frame is a row, each particle a product. Spin, camera angle, and transparency are adjustable from the toolbar inside fullscreen.

Phase shows the same data as an animated chord diagram. Reticulum superimposes all frames at once — the full orbit. Antikythera mode is a live product calculator: drag nodes to any octave position and watch all products shift simultaneously. Atlas maps prime composition across the octave by omega order.

Colour encodes inflection proximity. Size follows colour when the INF bias slider is engaged. Transparency, dot shape, and point size are adjustable from the Tools and Colour panels.

The Original Mechanism

The Antikythera mechanism is a 2,000-year-old bronze computer recovered from a Roman shipwreck in 1901. Gear-tooth ratios encoded celestial periods, turning orbital commensurability into a mechanical calculation. This tool is named for the same ambition: making the arithmetic of ratios navigable.

What It Does

Integers in [2^N, 2^(N+1)) are mapped to angles on a circle by their fractional base-2 logarithm. Multiplication becomes angular addition. Position encodes ratio, not magnitude. The choice of base 2 simplifies the continuum. The same structures hold for any other base, but will be missing both a comprehensive integer structure and a coherence with inversely-square phenomena.

The Matrix

Each cell holds the octave-reduced product of a row value and a column value. Octave reduction means: multiply, then divide or multiply by 2 until the result falls back into [2^N, 2^(N+1)). This is modular arithmetic on logarithms — addition mod 1 in log₂ space. Because every product lands back in the same octave, the entire multiplication table folds into a finite, navigable square. The colour at each cell encodes where in the octave that product sits, running from identity (2, or any octave boundary) through the inflection at √2. The gradient is not decorative: it is the only continuous, geometrically meaningful colouring of the octave. This is precisely the structure of pitch-class arithmetic in music theory — the octave as a circle, intervals as rotations, transposition as addition mod 12 — except here the modulus is continuous rather than twelve-fold.

Why √2

2^(1/2) is the geometric mean of the octave boundaries — symmetric under x ↦ 2/x, its own octave-complement. It is the inflection point of product density and the point of maximum harmonic tension. The colour gradient runs between 2 (identity) and 2^(1/2) (inflection) — the only two non-arbitrary positions in the octave.

In music, √2 is the exact frequency ratio of the tritone — the interval that divides the octave precisely in half. Equal temperament approximates it as six semitones (2^(6/12) = 2^(1/2)), making the tritone the only interval that is its own inversion. Its historical dissonance — the diabolus in musica — reflects its position at the point of maximum distance from the identity in log₂ space. Here it marks the same structural pivot: the inflection between the identity-dominated lower half and the octave-boundary-dominated upper half of the octave.

Antikythera Mode

A product-angle correspondence calculator. Nodes placed at octave positions; products computed between them. Moving one node changes all products simultaneously. Pairwise shows pair-product chords; Combined reduces all nodes to one product.

Views

· Zoetrope — 3D torus. Frame sweeps major axis; product sits on minor circle. Slider: 2.0 = torus, 1.0 = horn, 0.0 = sphere.

· Phase — product matrix animated row by row.

· Reticulum — all frames superimposed. Full multiplicative orbit.

· Antikythera — product-angle calculator. Drag nodes.

· Atlas — prime composition wheel. Boxes encode omega order. Atlas mode: primes on concentric rings by octave N.

Potential

The marketability of this program is significant, mainly in terms of its visuals. If anything, this could produce a wide array of natural-looking, continuous forms that are pleasing to the eye — screensavers and backgrounds to be accompanied by music or effects. The customizability of this framework can offer a multitude of smooth environments.

The Original Mechanism

The Antikythera mechanism is a 2,000-year-old bronze computer recovered from a Roman shipwreck in 1901. Gear-tooth ratios encoded celestial periods, turning orbital commensurability into a mechanical calculation. This tool is named for the same ambition: making the arithmetic of ratios navigable.

What It Does

Integers in [2^N, 2^(N+1)) are mapped to angles on a circle by their fractional base-2 logarithm. Multiplication becomes angular addition. Position encodes ratio, not magnitude. The choice of base 2 simplifies the continuum. The same structures hold for any other base, but will be missing both a comprehensive integer structure and a coherence with inversely-square phenomena.

The Matrix

Each cell holds the octave-reduced product of a row value and a column value. Octave reduction means: multiply, then divide or multiply by 2 until the result falls back into [2^N, 2^(N+1)). This is modular arithmetic on logarithms — addition mod 1 in log₂ space. Because every product lands back in the same octave, the entire multiplication table folds into a finite, navigable square. The colour at each cell encodes where in the octave that product sits, running from identity (2, or any octave boundary) through the inflection at √2. The gradient is not decorative: it is the only continuous, geometrically meaningful colouring of the octave. This is precisely the structure of pitch-class arithmetic in music theory — the octave as a circle, intervals as rotations, transposition as addition mod 12 — except here the modulus is continuous rather than twelve-fold.

Why √2

2^(1/2) is the geometric mean of the octave boundaries — symmetric under x ↦ 2/x, its own octave-complement. It is the inflection point of product density and the point of maximum harmonic tension. The colour gradient runs between 2 (identity) and 2^(1/2) (inflection) — the only two non-arbitrary positions in the octave.

In music, √2 is the exact frequency ratio of the tritone — the interval that divides the octave precisely in half. Equal temperament approximates it as six semitones (2^(6/12) = 2^(1/2)), making the tritone the only interval that is its own inversion. Its historical dissonance — the diabolus in musica — reflects its position at the point of maximum distance from the identity in log₂ space. Here it marks the same structural pivot: the inflection between the identity-dominated lower half and the octave-boundary-dominated upper half of the octave.

Antikythera Mode

A product-angle correspondence calculator. Nodes placed at octave positions; products computed between them. Moving one node changes all products simultaneously. Pairwise shows pair-product chords; Combined reduces all nodes to one product.

Views

· Zoetrope — 3D torus. Frame sweeps major axis; product sits on minor circle. Slider: 2.0 = torus, 1.0 = horn, 0.0 = sphere.

· Phase — product matrix animated row by row.

· Reticulum — all frames superimposed. Full multiplicative orbit.

· Antikythera — product-angle calculator. Drag nodes.

· Atlas — prime composition wheel. Boxes encode omega order. Atlas mode: primes on concentric rings by octave N.

Potential

The marketability of this program is significant, mainly in terms of its visuals. If anything, this could produce a wide array of natural-looking, continuous forms that are pleasing to the eye — screensavers and backgrounds to be accompanied by music or effects. The customizability of this framework can offer a multitude of smooth environments.
